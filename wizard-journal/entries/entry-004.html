The Rescue of Petra Lang

Fourth Day of Mirtul, Year 1492 DR

Our search through the warrens for the missing Lang sister continued today, though I confess the endless tunnels were beginning to wear on my patience. The damp air carries an unsettling scent that seems to seep into one's very bones, and the constant scratching echoes from unseen passages kept us all on edge.

We were ambushed yet again by what I can only describe as a terrifying swarm of rats – their numbers seemed almost supernatural. The wisdom of retreat became immediately apparent, and we fled deeper into the warren system, my robes catching on the rough stone walls as we scrambled through narrow passages.

Tactical Note: In confined spaces, area-effect spells prove far more valuable than single-target incantations. I must remember to prepare Burning Hands for future underground expeditions.

Fortune, however, smiled upon us during our escape. We discovered another group of the vile creatures gathered around something extraordinary – a massive piece of meteoric rock that pulsed with an otherworldly energy. More importantly, huddled beside this celestial fragment was our quarry: the Lang sister herself, very much alive despite her harrowing ordeal.

The battle that followed tested every ounce of our skill and coordination. The rats seemed almost protective of both the meteorite and their captive, fighting with a ferocity I had not previously witnessed in such creatures. But we prevailed, and both Petra Lang and the remarkable stone were freed from their captivity.

Petra proved to be a gracious soul, expressing heartfelt gratitude for her rescue. The relief in her eyes when we mentioned her brother was quite moving. Our journey to Shepherd's Gate felt considerably lighter with our mission nearing completion.

Brother Lang's joy at our success was evident, and he honored our agreement with the promised 250 gold pieces. An interesting development arose from our conversation with the Hooded Lanterns – while we lack formal transit rights through their gate, they have offered us a form of protection. Should we ever find ourselves pursued by enemies, they would grant us sanctuary and deal with any who would harm us. A valuable alliance, though I hope we never need to test it.

The ever-practical Petra suggested we might find buyers for our meteoric prize at the Amethyst Academy or perhaps among other scholarly institutions. The commercial possibilities are intriguing, though I find myself more interested in the artifact's magical properties.

Relief washed over me when we learned that Loric survived the earlier ambush and has taken refuge at the garrison in the inner city. One less weight upon my conscience.

Our departure for Emberwood Village began well enough, but was marred by an encounter with the Silver Order – if such scoundrels deserve the title of "order" at all. These highway bandits had the audacity to attempt robbery under the guise of official inspection. Their transparent avarice and crude intimidation tactics left me quite disgusted. We managed to avoid their extortion, but I fear others may not be so fortunate.

Personal Reflection: The corruption that festers in organizations meant to serve justice continues to astound me. Perhaps there is truth to my master's warnings about the moral decay that accompanies unchecked power.

Tonight finds us in Emberwood Village, where Ziri has taken upon herself the task of negotiating the sale of some smaller fragments of our meteoric treasure. Meanwhile, Euphoria and I have claimed a quiet corner of the inn for what promises to be a fascinating evening of study. The main meteorite sits before us now, its surface seeming to shift and dance in the candlelight.

I can hardly contain my excitement for what secrets this celestial visitor might reveal under proper magical scrutiny.
