Mysterious Meetings and Sacred Relics

Seventh Day of Kythorn, Year 1492 DR

An intriguing day filled with unexpected opportunities and moral quandaries. We received word that someone called BlackJack Mel wished to meet with us. I confess I had never heard the name before, though Ziri seemed to recognize it immediately. The way her eyes lit up with both excitement and wariness tells me there's more history there than she's letting on.

Our first order of business was far more straightforward – finally finding a buyer for that peculiar meteorite we've been carrying. The Red Lion Hotel proved to be exactly the sort of establishment where members of the Amethyst Academy might conduct their business. After some negotiations, we managed to secure quite a favorable deal: 1000 gold pieces and a second-level spell scroll. The buyer seemed particularly interested in the meteorite's unusual magical resonance, which only confirms my suspicions about its otherworldly origins.

Personal Achievement: While the others ventured to the Skull and Sword taphouse to meet this mysterious BlackJack contact, I remained behind to focus on my studies. I successfully transcribed Crown of Madness into my spellbook – a delicate process that required hours of careful preparation and precise incantation work.

The meeting at the taphouse proved far more significant than expected. This BlackJack Mel, it turns out, has connections to the Queen's men and presented us with what can only be described as a morally complex proposition. We've been tasked with acquiring the Scepter of Saint Vitruvio from the Chapel of Saint Brenna in Drakkenheim.

The word "rob" was used quite explicitly, which sits uneasily with me. While I understand the practical necessities of our profession, the thought of stealing from a sacred place dedicated to Saint Brenna gives me pause. I find myself wondering about the true nature of this artifact and why the Queen's agents would want it so desperately. Perhaps there's more to this scepter than mere religious significance.

Daven, predictably, spent his time at the gambling tables rather than concerning himself with the political implications of our new assignment. Sometimes I envy his ability to focus on simple pleasures while the rest of us grapple with these larger mysteries.
