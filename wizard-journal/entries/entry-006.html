The Scepter's Shadow

Seventh Day of Kythorn, Year 1492 DR

Today brought revelations that still leave my mind reeling. We witnessed something that challenged everything I thought I knew about the boundaries between life and death. Saint Brenna – that enigmatic figure whose true nature grows more mysterious by the day – performed what can only be described as a resurrection ritual, calling forth armed undead from the depths of the ancient tomb beneath our feet.

The sight was both terrifying and fascinating. The way the bones reassembled themselves, sinew and shadow weaving together to create mockeries of their former lives... I find myself torn between scholarly curiosity and deep unease. What manner of power does this Saint Brenna truly wield?

Our subsequent planning session was delightfully thorough, if I may say so. It's refreshing to work with companions who appreciate the value of careful preparation. Ziri proved particularly insightful as we devised our approach to acquiring the scepter – a plan that, thankfully, succeeded despite the complications that arose.

Observation: The scepter emanates a peculiar magical aura that seems to shift between schools of magic. My preliminary examination suggests transmutation elements, though there may be layers of enchantment I haven't yet detected.

Unfortunately, our exit was far from clean. The paladins of the Silver Order spotted us on our way out – their vigilance is truly impressive, if inconvenient for those of us engaged in... shall we say, morally ambiguous acquisition work. We made haste back to town, hearts pounding and minds racing.

Calithea's appraisal skills proved invaluable once we reached safety. She estimates the scepter's worth at approximately 2,500 to 3,000 gold pieces, potentially more to the right collector. Naturally, we attempted to renegotiate our original agreement of 250 gold per person – hardly fair compensation for such a valuable find. While our attempts at haggling were received well enough, it became clear that our employer had no intention of increasing the payment beyond the agreed 1,000 gold total.

Still, the mission wasn't without its rewards. We've secured what appears to be a step toward gaining an audience with the Queen of Thieves herself – the shadowy leader of The Queen's Men faction. The prospect both thrills and unnerves me.

Perhaps most intriguingly, Calithea has begun investigating how our contact at the Skull and Sword knew of her noble heritage. The question haunts me: how does this mysterious figure know about my past? There are currents here deeper than we initially realized, and I suspect we've only begun to glimpse the true scope of the web we've stumbled into.

On a lighter note, Daven has acquired what he calls "Devil's Luck" – a magical finger item of some sort. I've yet to examine it properly, but knowing Daven's... particular interests, I suspect it will prove either remarkably useful or spectacularly dangerous. Possibly both.
